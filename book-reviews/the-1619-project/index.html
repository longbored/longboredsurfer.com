This collection of writings presents a thought-provoking perspective, and might be new to (and uncomfortable for) people less familiar with some of the distasteful aspects of American history and potential modern implications.

Slavery officially played a role in America's history for nearly 250 years, but the impact continues to this day. The book highlights some of those current areas. I was familiar with most, but not all, of the areas discussed in this book. The book also draws on comparisons to other nations and the approaches to historical injustice, noting that different societies have taken different paths in addressing past wrongs - it's a comparison I've heard before, but I appreciated the reminder, and found myself agreeing with it more this time. Slavery was wrong. As a nation, we haven't done a good job of addressing that wrong, and still collectively suffer from that.

Some of the essays do a better job than others. The overall collection of topics discussed is helpful, and may save time for those interested in the topic, but not wanting to commit to several books on the various topics.
