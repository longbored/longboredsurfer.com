I'm cautious to share an opinion of Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn and his writing. I feel like a nobody, and Solzhenitsyn feels like a giant with far-reaching influence. As I understand it, this was the only book he was allowed to publish in the Soviet Union itself, and it was later disallowed. It's not earth shattering to me 50+ years after its original publication, but the insight into the gulags was devoured (and later done so illegally). I can only imagine how it would be to live in a gulag, and to deal with the things Ivan (or Solzhenitsyn himself) dealt with. His descriptions were vivid, and only improved my imagination of such a life. "Old Man Whiskers" - a great nickname for their nation's leader.
