"Neverwhere"

by Neil Gaiman

  • Read on 2026-05-28
  • Rating: ️️️️️
  • Format: 🎧 (13 hours 48 minutes)

Fans of Neil Gaiman love Neil Gaiman. I purchased the audiobook for this years ago, and it sat in my library unlistened-to ever since. I had the chance to explore this book with a book club, and I'm glad I took the chance.

My brother described the book well to me, in saying that although it is a "fantasy" novel, as judged by the simplest classification, it's more than that. I'd add the tag of "mystery" and "suspense" at the same time. The protagonist, Richard Mayhew, stops to help an injured woman, and ends up being pulled into a pseudo-parallel version of London. Down there, unique characteristics, traits, and abilities are had by the inhabitants. The goal is to learn who has it in for the injured woman, and to potentially regain what Richard once had.

This isn't my first experience with Gaiman's writing, nor do I think it'll be my last. Sometimes it feels like he's simply being creative to be creative - not that it needs to make sense, or even fit in. But some of that creativity I enjoy quite a bit. Sometimes, I'm confused.
