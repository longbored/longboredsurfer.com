"For Cause and Comrades: Why Men Fought in the Civil War"

by James M. McPherson

  • Read on 2026-02-24
  • Rating: ️️️️️
  • Format: 🎧 (9 hours 13 minutes)

As the subtitle clearly informs you - this is a book focused on why men fought in the Civil War. It was extensively researched, and I appreciated the copious amount of personal correspondance that was referenced. Unsurprisingly, the reasons why they fought varied wildly. Duty played a large role for so many of the early volunteers. For the North, it was interesting to see the shift from pure patriotism to being much more anti-slavery - and how witnessing slavery helped to strongly influence that sentiment.

Both sides were so disgusted by the actual gruesomeness of battle. And similarly strong feelings around ever being labeled a coward. "Bone spurs" not needed.

And with the amount of death in the war, it was again sobering how many of those letters were followed up with the details of that soldier's demise.
