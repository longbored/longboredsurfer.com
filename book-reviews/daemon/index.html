Daemon

Published on 1970-01-01 by Daniel Suarez

Don't read chapter 4. If you start it, once they get to the rave, just skip to the next chapter. I'm serious. Having been warned of it, but still catching a glimpse, it's truly awful (explicit, depraved, and horrendously pornographic). I'm glad I could get that out of the way, because otherwise it was an enjoyable book, though quite profane.

A genius programmer has died, and built a self-running/triggering piece of software that attempts to implement a meritocracy across the entire world, recruiting people to its cause. What a boring description of an otherwise engaging book. This felt like a good 'ol Michael Crichton sci-fi novel - taking science and technology we're already familiar with, maybe introducing things to us we weren't aware of, and pushing it just a bit farther into the area of fiction, where you're not quite sure what's real/possible, and what's completely false.

Despite chapter four, and one other scene of marital infidelity, I enjoyed the book - setting up heroes and villains - but never quite sure which is which.
