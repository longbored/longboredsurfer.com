This was better than the second book in this series, but not as good as the first book. The series started out with great world building in a sci-fi series, took a hard right turn into endless conversations for the second book, and was a mix of the two for the third. I might be able to be talked into another from this series, but I'm somewhat relieved to see any other currently-released books are prequels (and maybe more in line with the first).
