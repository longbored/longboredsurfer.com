I continue to lament the relations between the Native Americans and the European settlers. Simply put: they had different standards, goals, and expectations. And when one of those areas might have met, the others didn't, and conflict continued. The casual cruelty displayed by so many remains startling to my modern sensibilities. The Comanches seemed impressive. The Parkers had quite the story. I enjoyed reading the latter more than the portrayal of the former. I feel that most non-Native American writers fall short in their portrayal of the continent's first inhabitants. By no means was the author disrespectful, but the portrayal felt narrow.
