This is an interesting view into CRISPR, and how the discovery of it, and the associated subsequent enablement of gene editing has come about. I'm not a scientist, but found the book to be understandable and approachable. It's interesting to see how functionality that has been in play so long in nature has been identified, and used for our collective benefit. While I agree that it's moral to remove diseases from bloodlines, I acknowledge it's a very slippery slope of what gets modified beyond that. Regardless, I enjoyed the book. I've enjoyed multiple of Isaacson's books, and suspect I'll read more of them in the future.
