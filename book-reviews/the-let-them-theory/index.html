(I listened to this for a book club at work.) Last weekend I was sick, and listened to this entire book in a day. That's hardly the appropriate amount of time to ingest the material and put it into practice with any efficacy.

On the plus side, Mel Robbins acknowledges the commonalities between her theory. I have a couple children making the transition to adulthood, and as I am learning more and more, I have less and less influence on, or control over them. Robbins shares some ideas that are easily actionable, and would likely make a great positive impact. There are some things in the book I needed to hear. I also wanted a variety of others in my life to read the book both for their benefit, and our mutual benefit. Robbins reads her own book, and does a really good job with it - you can hear the emotion and genuine feeling she has.

And to nitpick, not everything needs to be so superlative-laden. It felt like too many things were described as ground-breaking, revolutionary, mind-blowing, earth-shattering, or some other similar adjective. It also felt like a variety of the research/study examples cited could have used more. The book could have been much shorter, since it felt like some points were belabored or repeated.
