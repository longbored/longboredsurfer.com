"Mark Twain"

by Ron Chernow

  • Read on 2026-04-09
  • Rating: ️️️️️
  • Format: 🎧 (44 hours 35 minutes)

I enjoy Ron Chernow's writing as a biographer. He's thorough, yet keeps the subject and timeline moving along smoothly. But sometimes (as has happened in this book) he fixates on a negative point about a person, to the point that it's almost uncomfortable. I don't expect a biographer to skip over the unsavory parts about someone, but I don't think I need as much detail, or belaboring of the disagreement with someone's ways.

Twain himself was surprisingly progressive in a number of ways. He could be generous to causes that needed an advocate of his stature. At the same time, his family seemed to have constant health issues - something that influenced his travel and temperament. And really, he was a horrible business man. He could be cantankerous, opinionated, foolhardy, profligate, and willfully ignorant.

If somebody did now what Mark Twain did then, he'd certainly be labeled as a creepy old man (and probably would get a certain label, despite always having chaperones, and no sexual contact). He needed his wife to live longer, since she seemed to help reign him in, in a variety of ways.

But he could tell a story, and turn a clever phrase. He captured live audiences with his quick wit, unique delivery, and quirky accounts. He was immensely well-traveled, and wrote stories that captured an era. He is STILL highly quotable, for better or worse. His comments have offended me, you, and most other people. But he seemed to be true to himself, and making his name(s) be as well known to as many people as he could, because he loved the attention and the money.

I don't feel like I ever need to read another book about Twain again.
