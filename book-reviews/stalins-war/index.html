Yes - from at least one perspective, Russia was the biggest winner of the worst war this world has ever seen. It doesn't change the fact that Stalin was a loser. Russia annexed countries, starved countless people to death (including upwards of 10K per day in Ukraine), murdered millions, got invaded by a different loser, duped Roosevelt into a truly astounding amount of goods and materials by way of the Lend-Lease Act (really - SO MUCH more than I realized before) for nothing in return, dragged their feet with regards to Japan, and ultimately championed a government that failed and is now lead by yet another loser.

I was hoping for new insight into Russia and World War II. I got it, but somehow lowered my opinion of them (and others) by way of the well-detailed actions described in this book. Don't bother with this book unless you're really into World War II. Well-written. Well-researched. Too many details about Lend-Lease aspects (though it really is staggering how much Russia got - what an incredible boost, both during the war, and substantially helping them for after it).

Lastly, the author repeated the nickname of "the Vozhd" so many times, it was comical. I wasn't previously familiar with that name/title, and after its over-usage in this book (and absence or much lighter use elsewhere), I'm more inclined to think the author simply liked that name, as opposed to it being in prevalent usage.
