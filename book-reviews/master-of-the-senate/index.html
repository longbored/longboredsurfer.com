Master of the Senate

Published on 1970-01-01 by Robert A. Caro

This volume covers the majority of Johnson's twelve years in the Senate. I'm glad this volume exists. Finally one of my main reservations about Lyndon Johnson was articulated in a simple way: he was cruel. And while he continues to have many reprehensible qualities (including well-documented racist behaviors), him championing the first passage of a civil rights bill (no matter how meager) in the Senate, feels really significant. Anything to finally break the 80+ year streak of nothing... and finally something.

Furthermore, I love the additional insight this volume gives to the workings of the Senate, and a view of America's history during this time. Caro continues to do such a great job of establishing the appropriate context to better understand it all. Caro's writing: five out of five. Johnson himself, at this point: some characteristics deserve a zero out of five, and others a five out of five.
