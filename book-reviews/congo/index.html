I miss Michael Crichton. Congo helps show part of the reason why. Crichton had a gift for bringing me up to speed in the book's subject. I'd feel knowledgeable enough at each step of the process. You don't know anything about apes? Fine. Now you do. Apes who know sign language? You're welcome. Moore's Law? Now you do. And yet.... at some point in the education process of me as a reader, he'd quietly slip into fiction. He'd stretch the truth on something, and yet do it so imperceptibly, that I'd still think we're completely in reality. So, when things started to go sideways, I never knew if they were things I should actually be concerned about or not.

This one wasn't earth-shatteringly good, but I still enjoyed it.
