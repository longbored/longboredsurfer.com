Honestly, I don't know if the book is a 5-star book, but Lincoln was a 5-star individual. I loved the presentation of his time period, and how (and in what ways) he was unique, as well as similar to those around him. While we can focus on the racist-by-today's-standards comments he made, the strength of character displayed by this amazing man stands as a true high-water mark I fail to see attained by others in similar roles.

He was taught principles when he was young. He saw injustices first-hand. He knew things could/should be better. He repeatedly referenced his conscience, and how he'd feel standing before his Maker if he didn't follow that conscience. I'm not sure many politicians these days can pronounce conscience, let alone acknowledge our Maker. I captured so many quotes from this book, and am comfortable I missed many others. Still - I'll refrain from referencing any of them, and encourage to read this book instead.
