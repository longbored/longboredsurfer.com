A whirlwind FBI investigation following a series of event that seem too much of a coincidence.

I by far preferred the first book, but I sure enjoy the world the author has built. Near-future sci-fi, with people able to experience the world and control remote robotic bodies to continue living a normal life. Although the author is focused on crime investigations in a world like this, it has potential to be a lot of other things. Through the book he hints at things that would be commonplace in such a world, and those details are enjoyable.
