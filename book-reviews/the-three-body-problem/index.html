The Three-Body Problem

Published on 1970-01-01 by Liu Cixin

This was enjoyable. It's interesting to me how often some science fiction books come back to some moral issue at the core of the book. "Let's invent all sorts of other things, so we can simply make the reader ponder an issue." That said, I enjoyed what they created in the book. I'm curious to read the follow-up books.

It feels a little tricky to describe the book well without spoiling it. The characters themselves are originally in the dark, so why should the potential reader know more the characters? A mix of science, intrigue, video games, and moral issues, all mixed into a book you're likely to enjoy if you like science fiction books.
