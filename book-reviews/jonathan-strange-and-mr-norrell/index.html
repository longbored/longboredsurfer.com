How to summarize this book? It's written historically, acting as if magic were a matter of fact, and through the numerous (and extensive) cross-references, you were looking to get a better understand of magic's return to England, by way of Mr. Norrell (of course), and his apprentice-turned-semi-adversary Jonathan Strange.

It starts slow, and some of the details seem irrelevant. Admittedly, however, the world and vision of it all are rather rewarding in hindsight. Clearly Susanna Clarke has a gift for writing stories, and has done a wonderful job here. Despite how slow it _feels_ like it starts, so much happens in the last quarter of the book that immediately after finishing it, I immediately went back to re-read it from that point onward.

Between the two characters in the title, I also enjoyed the gentleman with the thistledown hair, and especially Stephen Black.
