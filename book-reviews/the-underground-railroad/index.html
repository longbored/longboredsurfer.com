"The Underground Railroad"

by Colson Whitehead

  • Read on 2026-04-18
  • Rating: ️️️️️
  • Format: 🎧 (10 hours 43 minutes)

I'll be honest: I finished this book confused. The narrative shifts in ways that defy easy explanation, and for a while I wasn't sure if that was a failure of the storytelling or a failure of my reading. It was neither.

Each state Cora travels through isn't meant to be a place so much as a face, a different expression of the same American machinery of racism. This isn't a linear escape story. It's a tour through forms oppression has taken.

What landed hardest was the disorientation itself. The groundlessness. That was the lived reality of enslaved people: no safe place, no stable ground, no way to predict what form danger would take next. The confusion I felt as a reader was a fraction of what they carried as a life. It reminded me of how Alex Haley shared Roots - just as things were becoming familiar, an entire world shifted, and questions linger.

The ending gave me something to hold onto. Cora on that cart, moving toward an uncertain horizon, felt like digging out. Not triumphant. Not resolved. Just moving forward, which was sometimes all there was.

Worth reading, and thinking about.
