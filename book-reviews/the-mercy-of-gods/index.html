Cheap and easy sci-fi. There's so much world-building going on here that it can be a little daunting to keep track of the huge variety of newness all over. Some of the questions and struggles the people (who are captive (as the title of the series lets you know)) ask and confront are things I'm not sure I'd be mature enough to ask. Complex interpersonal relationship. Nuanced inter-societal struggles. A huge enemy. This is set for the long-haul. But, if the author's other major series is any indicator, that's not surprising.
