I read this for a book club. I liked the description given by somebody else in the club - people handle loss in different ways. Cheryl Strayed partially handled that loss and grief by hiking the Pacific Coast Trail, and writing this book. I'm glad she documented it, if for nothing else than handling her own mental health. The writing didn't stand out to me as anything particularly amazing nor awful - her style stayed out of the way of the events.

The Eagle Scout in me screamed in horror at her preparation, and lack there-of. It felt appalling that she could find even the level of success she found, with her preparation. In some ways she was highly prepared, and in others she acknowledged her nearly willful ignorance about some areas - areas where experts fully warned her. On the other hand, maybe my own analysis paralysis would prevent me from ever pursuing something like this (if I actually wanted to do this). To her credit, she still made things work. Lastly, I got entirely too much information about someone's sexual thoughts and activities.
